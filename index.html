Thesis 1: Strong Markets Get Stronger

History shows that when the SPY has gained over 10% by the end of October (an event that occurred 8 times since 2006), both the broad market and the retail sector tend to continue climbing into the year's end.

In these 8 instances, the Retail ETF (XRT) posted a slightly higher average return for the rest of the year (RoY) than the S&P 500 (SPY).

Thesis 2: The Retail "Catch-Up" Trade

Our core thesis identifies a specific, powerful pattern. In years where the SPY was strong (>10% YTD) but XRT was lagging (flat or negative), the retail sector has historically "caught up" with dramatic outperformance in the final two months. The 2025 setup (SPY +16.7%, XRT +2.1%) perfectly matches this historical precedent.

Section 2: The 2025 Strategy

Based on this analysis, we will adopt a bullish stance with a specific tactical tilt toward the retail sector, positioning the fund to capture both broad market momentum and the anticipated "catch-up" rally in XRT.
